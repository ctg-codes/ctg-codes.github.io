Introduction

This service-learning project presents a comprehensive healthcare reform plan designed to address major challenges in the U.S. healthcare system. The project focuses on improving affordability, expanding access to care, and increasing overall system efficiency through structured policy proposals. By analyzing existing gaps and proposing realistic reforms, this project highlights ways to create a more equitable and sustainable healthcare system.

Motivation / Significance

  • Healthcare costs in the United States continue to rise, making care unaffordable for many individuals and families.
  • Access to essential services, especially preventive and mental health care, remains limited in many communities.
  • Administrative complexity and inefficiencies increase costs and reduce the quality of patient care.
  • This project aims to propose practical solutions that can improve health outcomes and reduce disparities within the community.

Method / Approach / Activities

  • Researched current healthcare policies and identified key issues affecting cost, access, and efficiency.
  • Analyzed common challenges such as insurance complexity, high prescription drug prices, and limited preventive care.
  • Organized findings into major reform categories to create a structured and easy-to-understand plan.
  • Developed policy recommendations aimed at improving healthcare delivery and outcomes.

Results / Outcomes

Lower and Stabilize Costs

  • Require price transparency across hospitals and insurers
  • Negotiate lower prescription drug prices
  • Limit surprise billing and facility fees

Fix the Insurance System

  • Simplify insurance plan structures
  • Strengthen prior authorization rules
  • Expand public insurance options

Expand Coverage Gaps

  • Broaden Medicaid expansion
  • Increase subsidies for middle-income families
  • Expand preventive care coverage

Focus on Prevention

  • Invest in primary care workforce
  • Promote early screening and chronic care
  • Integrate mental health into primary care

Prescription Drug Reform

  • Cap essential medication costs
  • Reform pharmacy benefit managers
  • Encourage generic drug competition

Hospital System Efficiency

  • Improve cost transparency
  • Shift to value-based care models
  • Support rural hospital funding

Mental Health Reform

  • Enforce mental health parity
  • Expand behavioral health services
  • Integrate screening into primary care

Administrative Simplification

  • Standardize billing systems
  • Improve electronic health record interoperability
  • Reduce paperwork burden

Public Health Investment

  • Strengthen disease prevention systems
  • Expand vaccination programs
  • Improve environmental health standards

Conclusion

Through this service-learning project, I gained a deeper understanding of the challenges within the healthcare system and the importance of policy-based solutions. I learned how factors such as cost, access, and administrative processes are interconnected and impact patient outcomes. This project helped strengthen my ability to analyze real-world issues and develop structured solutions that can benefit communities.
